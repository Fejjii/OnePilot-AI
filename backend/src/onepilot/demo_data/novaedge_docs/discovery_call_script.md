# NovaEdge Solutions — Discovery Call Script

Discovery calls last **60 minutes**. Use this script. It is not a strict word-for-word reading;
it is a checklist that you must cover.

## Pre-call (5 minutes before)
- Review the inbound form, the LinkedIn profile of the attendee, and the company's website.
- Open the NovaEdge demo workspace (`org_demo_novaedge`).
- Confirm the prospect's company is in our ICP (`sales_playbook.md`). If not, plan to
  qualify out gracefully.

## 0:00 — Welcome (2 min)
> "Hi {name}, thanks for the time. Quick agenda: 5 minutes on you, 30 minutes on the problem
> you're trying to solve, 10 minutes on whether NovaEdge is a fit, then 15 minutes for your
> questions. Sound good?"

## 0:02 — About the prospect (5 min)
- "Tell me about your role and how the company makes money today."
- Listen for: team size, growth stage, current pain.

## 0:07 — Problem statement (15 min)
Ask, in order:

1. "What's the one workflow that, if it ran itself, would change your team's week?"
2. "Walk me through the current process — who does it, how long does it take, and where does it break?"
3. "What does success look like at 90 days? Be specific."
4. "What have you tried before? Why didn't it work?"
5. "Who else on your team would have to be involved?"

Capture quotes verbatim. They are gold for the proposal.

## 0:22 — Constraints (8 min)
- "What's your budget envelope for this kind of project?"
- "What's your decision process? Who signs?"
- "What's your timeline? Soft or hard?"
- "Any compliance constraints I should know about?"

If the prospect is reluctant to share budget, give a price range from `pricing_plans.md`.
Specifically: "Most customers like you start with a $4,900 Pilot or $14,900 Standard
Implementation. Does that bracket sound workable, or way off?"

## 0:30 — Show, don't tell (10 min)
- Walk through the relevant package from `services_overview.md`.
- Show one screen from the demo workspace.
- Anchor on outcomes, not features. "Here's what your inbox looks like at 60% triage
  automation."

## 0:40 — Fit check (10 min)
Be honest. If they're a poor fit:

- "Honestly, you might be better served by {alternative}. We're great for {ICP}, less great
  for {anti-ICP}. Want me to introduce you to {partner}?"

If they're a fit:

- Confirm next steps and dates.
- Confirm the **economic buyer**.
- Confirm the success criteria for a Pilot.

## 0:50 — Q&A (10 min)
Use the FAQ patterns:

- Pricing → `pricing_plans.md`
- Security → `security_policy.md`, `data_privacy_policy.md`
- Hallucinations / confidence → `ai_usage_policy.md`
- Comparisons → `objection_handling.md`

If asked something not covered: write it down, say "I'll come back to you within 24 hours
with a precise answer." Never make it up.

## 1:00 — Close
- Restate the next step.
- Confirm who is responsible for it.
- Confirm the date.
- Send the recap email within 24 hours (template in `email_templates.md`).

## After the call (30 min)
- Update HubSpot:
  - Stage advanced to Discovery → Scoping (if criteria met).
  - Notes pasted verbatim where possible.
  - Risks tagged.
- Schedule follow-ups.
- If the deal is a clear no, mark Closed-Lost / reason. Do not chase.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Talking more than the prospect.** You should be quiet for ~60% of the call.
- **Demo-loading.** A discovery call is not a demo. If they want a demo, schedule the
  separate demo call and use `demo_call_checklist.md`.
- **Solutioning too early.** Resist quoting a price before the problem is clear.
- **Skipping next-step ownership.** "We'll be in touch" is not a next step.

## When to End Early
- The prospect is not the buyer or champion → end at 30 min, ask for an intro.
- The use case is clearly out of scope → end at 30 min, refer out.
- The prospect's hostility or red flags appear → end professionally and disqualify.
